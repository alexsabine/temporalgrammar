About

This website houses the CRR framework - a coarse-grained mathematical model exploring coherence, rupture, and regeneration dynamics across living and non-living systems. The framework emerges from interdisciplinary research spanning process philosophy, Active Inference, and complexity science.

Alexander Sabine is an interdisciplinary researcher with a background in Child Development, Cognitive Science, and English Literature (particularly William Blake scholarship). His doctoral research at the University of Portsmouth focuses on participatory action mapping paradigms. He serves on the Board of Directors for the Active Inference Institute (2026) and is a Fellow of the Higher Education Academy (Advance HE). He holds EYTS status and has degrees spanning science, art and humanities.

Contact: alexander@active-inference.co.uk
